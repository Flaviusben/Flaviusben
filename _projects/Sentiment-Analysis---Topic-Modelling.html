Topic Modeling with LDA

# Set seed for reproducibility
set.seed(1234)

# First: extract the necessary columns and clean the text by removing numbers and punctuation.
review_data <- high_importance_reviews_clean %>%
  select(reviewerID, overall, reviewText) %>%
  dplyr::mutate(text = gsub(x = reviewText, pattern = "[0-9]+|[[:punct:]]|\\(.*\\)", replacement = ""))

# Second: matrix format where rows are documents (reviews) and columns are features (words)
tokens <- tokens(review_data$text, remove_punct = TRUE)

dfm <- dfm(tokens) %>%
  dfm_remove(stopwords("english")) %>%
  dfm_trim(min_termfreq = 10)

# Convert DFM for use in topic modeling
dfmlda <- convert(dfm, to = "topicmodels")

The Document-Feature Matrix (DFM) transforms the text data into a structured format where each row represents a document (in our case, reviews) and each column represents a term. This matrix format is necessary for applying topic modeling algorithms, as it quantifies the presence of terms across documents, enabling the identification of patterns and topics.

What are the limitations of using LDA for topic modeling? results depend heavily on how text is cleaned, tokenized, and filtered. LDA assumes topics are represented by word distributions, which may oversimplify complex relationships in text. The coherence of topics often requires domain expertise to validate.

Conclusion

This analysis has provided a comprehensive view of customer sentiment and feedback through the lens of Natural Language Processing. Sentiment analysis revealed the distribution of emotions within customer reviews, highlighting the prevalence of both positive sentiments like trust and joy, as well as negative emotions such as anger and sadness. This nuanced understanding allows for a more targeted approach to addressing customer concerns and reinforcing positive experiences.

Topic modeling, particularly with the VEM method, identified key recurring themes in customer feedback. These include specific product attributes like sound and speaker quality and camera performance, alongside broader issues related to device compatibility (e.g., with iPhone and tablet) and storage/connectivity (e.g., drive and USB issues). Notably, these topics were cross-validated with sentiment analysis and satisfaction scores, reinforcing their relevance and providing actionable insights.

The analysis also highlighted the potential for refining product categorization. For instance, the Technical feedback category, which presented collinearity issues in earlier modeling efforts, could be effectively decomposed into more granular subtopics such as battery life, software issues, and hardware problems. This refinement, inspired by the topic modeling results, would lead to more precise analysis and targeted interventions.

While LDA proved effective in identifying key topic-word relationships, its important to acknowledge that clustering methodologies could offer a complementary perspective. Clustering, potentially enhanced by techniques like embedding, could group documents based on their topic distributions, revealing higher-level patterns in customer feedback. Although our focus remained on topic-word relationships, future work could explore this avenue to further enrich the analysis.
